Point of Care Summary

POCS is our comprehensive dashboard that provides key clinical information including labs, visit notes, nutritional needs, allergies, medications, diet orders, anthropometry and more. Easily lookup historical values and identify trends.

Seamless Scheduling

Manage your patient records and calendars in one place. Because scheduling and documentation live within one system, you never have to toggle between programs. Build, edit, and view regular and custom schedules.

Nutrition Progress Notes

Make your notes based on the ADA's International Dietetics and Nutrition Terminology (IDNT). Review and analyze all data pertinent to clinical decision making from the same screen while inserting your assessments, nutrition diagnosis or interventions. Pull in various nutritional calculators with just one click.

Encounter View

Encounter view provides a quick summary of all charting activities performed during and after a visit. On follow up sessions easily scroll through previous encounters, or skip to any part, to see patient improvement.

Cliniwiz is a Cloud based EMR System for the Indian Physicians.

Cliniwiz Integrates practices, Simplifies Health care, allows Practice without boundaries

All-in-One

EMR | Practice management system | Patient Portal

Developed with the intention of Integrating Physicians and supporting them by giving a logistic support to Increase Revenue, Reduce Costs, Decrease Risks, Enhance Quality, Information Availability, Enhance Productivity, and Ensure Compliance.

India has an extensive primary care system of which the family doctor or general practitioner is the most important participant. This group however is not well organised, neither as individual care provider nor as joint professionals. As a result of this, the primary care profession is poorly protected and quality assurance to protect patients is poor, leading to market participation of large numbers of quacks. Another problem is that care delivered in the (cost-efficient) primary care system is not covered by medical or health insurance. This leads to (very) low tariffs for the primary care physician.

The Indian market is rapidly moving towards corporatization by Giant Business houses. It is time for the Physician to take control over this situation.
